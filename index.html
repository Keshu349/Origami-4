Origami Designs and Their Facts

Camel Origami

  • How to make a Camel

  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Chameleon Origami

  • How to make a Chameleon

  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • Many species of chameleon have horn-like bumps or crests on their heads.
  • Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.

Cicada Origami

  • How to make a Cicada

  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Cicadas are found throughout the U.S.A
  • Most cicadas appear every year in late June through August, while others emerge only every few years.

Panda Origami

  • How to make a Panda

  • Pandas' favorite food is bamboo shoots.
  • An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos, and can be about 1.5 meters long!
  • Pandas are usually born in August, because the panda's mating months are March to May and gestation is 3 to 5 months.

Pigeon Origami

  • How to make a Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities. They use a range of skills, such as using the sun as a guide and an internal ‘magnetic compass’.

Teddy-Bear Origami

  • How to make a Teddy-Bear

  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.
  • The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902
  • However, it was the 26th President of the US, Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt, who was the inspiration behind the first teddy bear.
